Jackson was a famous general during the War of 1812

A timeline of Andrew Jackson's life

  • 1767 - Born in the Waxhaws

  • 1787 - Passed the bar and moved to Jonesborough, Tennessee

  • 1788 - Apointed Solicitor of the Western District

  • 1796 - Elected as a delegate to the Tennessee constitutional convention

  • 1796 - Elected Tennessee's U.S. Representative

  • 1797 - Elected Tennessee's U.S. Senator

  • 1798 - Resigned Senatorship

  • 1798 - Appointed as judge of Tennessee Supreme Court

  • 1802 - Elected Major General of Tennessee militia

  • 1812 - Battle of New Orleans. Jackson's force defeated a larger British force with minimum casualties

  • 1817 - Led campaign against Seminole Indians, deposted Spanish governor of Florida

  • 1824 - Defeated in the presidential election by by John Quincy Adams. However Jackson's popularity skyrocketed

  • 1828 - Elected President

  • 1828 onwards - Led Indian removal and relocation movement

  • 1829 - Petticoat affair leads to a total overhall of Jackson's cabinet

  • 1832 - Nullification crisis comes to a head

  • 1832 - Vetoed rechartering of the Bank of the United States

  • 1832 - Re-elected President

  • 1835 - Survived assassination attempt by Richard Lawrence, an unemployed painter

  • 1836 - Passed the Specie Circular, leading to the Panic of 1837

  • 1845 - Died at Hermitage Plantation
